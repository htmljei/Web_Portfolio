Growing Up, Growing Strong

Childhood Years | Teenage Years | College Years

Childhood Years

1 I was born and raised in Quezon City, a city with a peaceful yet simple lifestyle. 2 My family lived in a small but cozy home in a quiet neighborhood. 3 My parents always encouraged me to focus on my studies. 4 I attended a public school within walking distance from our house. 5 Every morning, I woke up early and followed my usual routine. 6 During recess, I loved playing tag and hide and seek with my classmates. 7 Watching cartoons and anime made my afternoons exciting. 8 My mom often asked me to help with household chores. 9 Our family outings were usually to malls or parks on weekends. 10 I was not very social and had only a few friends. 11 I preferred staying at home playing video games or watching movies. 12 The streets of Quezon City were always busy. 13 My parents were strict, and I had to ask for permission to go outside. 14 I wasn’t involved in any extracurricular activities. 15 My childhood was simple, but I was content. 16 I always obeyed my parents and focused on my studies. 17 I never excelled in sports, but I enjoyed school. 18 Looking back, my childhood was peaceful and filled with happy moments. 19 Though ordinary, I appreciate my past experiences. 20 I realize now that simplicity made my childhood special.

Teenage Years

1 As a teenager, I was just an ordinary student. 2 I was not the best in my class, but I managed to pass all my subjects. 3 In senior high school, I chose ICT as my track because of my interest in computers. 4 Coding was initially difficult for me. 5 I often got distracted and spent more time playing games. 6 My father reminded me to focus, but I still stayed up late. 7 Sometimes, my friends and I studied together at coffee shops. 8 I was not active in school clubs or organizations. 9 I preferred spending my free time with friends rather than joining school events. 10 Street food such as fish balls and kwek-kwek became our favorites. 11 We enjoyed hanging out at malls during weekends. 12 My parents didn’t always allow me to go out. 13 Sometimes, I had to make excuses just to meet friends. 14 I managed to improve my grades before graduating. 15 Graduation day was a proud moment for my parents and me. 16 It felt rewarding to see their happy faces. 17 Senior high school passed by quickly. 18 Soon, I was preparing for my college journey. 19 I was nervous but also excited for the challenges ahead. 20 I promised myself that I would give my best in college.

College Years

1 Currently, I am a second-year BSIT student at University of the East. 2 The first year was tough because everything was new to me. 3 Learning programming languages was one of the biggest challenges. 4 Some subjects were difficult, but I put in extra effort. 5 Fortunately, I met great friends who helped me adjust. 6 We often studied together and worked on projects as a team. 7 After class, we enjoyed eating at small food stalls near the school. 8 Sometimes, we played online games to relieve stress. 9 College is definitely harder than high school. 10 I struggled with deadlines, exams, and challenging lessons. 11 However, I remind myself of why I chose this path. 12 I want to gain more technical knowledge and secure a good job. 13 Over time, I have learned better time management skills. 14 I still find some subjects difficult but continue to work hard. 15 Balancing study and social life is important. 16 On weekends, I go out with friends to relax. 17 College has been a tough but meaningful journey. 18 I believe that hard work will help me achieve my goals. 19 My dream is to work in the IT industry and support my family. 20 Although the road ahead is challenging, I am determined to succeed.
